Java Street Mapper

November, 2013

java, swing, gui

Project for The Science of Data Structures that required us to created a GUI in Java that finds the shortest path (using Dijkstra's algorithm) between two paths on the real road network in Monroe County, New York. Also calculates the minimum spanning tree for the network.

Click two points on the grid to find the shortest road distance between them, or click on one point and drag to find shortest distance as a live update. Click again to choose a new starting point. Right click to clear points.

Coordinates are shown in the bottom left, and distance between points in the bottom right. (Not quite sure what the units on these are, but the distance seems approximately accurate interpreted as hectometers... how odd)
